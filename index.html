Digital archive is only a pause between interfaces. The immediate interface before the web interface are the APIs created after the contents in the archive. This is still under process. So the web interface here goes partially hypothetical in the absence of a public API.

A cross look at the digital and traditional archives.

My only assets were the experience of composition held in the chaotic studio process as an artist and the newbie of technology. A problem for me is not a solution seeking venture and it is definitely not the nature of a linear process of programing. The conflict of these two processes needed to discover a momentum that is already registered in the history of my creative process. A revisit to some of my works identified this in a new perspective.

Project at the beginning was triggered by the photographer's contact sheets. This has the potential to convert elements on the filmstrip to user interface(ui).

Detail of Jyoti Bhatt's contact sheet from AAA.

But the architecture of web layout is already changing and it is no more a direct translation of static layout models. The columns and the new flexbox are addressing the responsive layout. One of the effective ways to put "Living Tradtions" series is into a grid layout. This also fall back to the contact sheet appearance. The content drives the layout. Few examples of of diplay which followed grid system is given below:

I realized there is no strict formula to design. I kept on moving from wireframes to frontend and reciprocating frequently. The design is also sensitive on colors and typography. At times they also took a central role in design. Finding a font is not a hunting rather is a process of inhabiting in the content.

Interface is designed to be device agnostic; it works with mouse, touch and motion events.
